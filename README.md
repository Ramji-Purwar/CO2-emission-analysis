# Global CO₂ Emissions Analysis

**Website:** [https://ramji-purwar.github.io/CO2-emission-analysis/](https://ramji-purwar.github.io/CO2-emission-analysis/)

## Overview
This repository contains a comprehensive, data-driven investigation into global CO₂ and greenhouse gas emissions. Developed as an exploratory data analysis project, it aims to answer complex questions about our changing climate through the lens of data. The analysis is built upon the highly granular **Our World in Data (OWID) CO₂ and Greenhouse Gas Emissions dataset**.

## Project Structure
The analysis is structured to guide readers logically from fundamental economic relationships to complex policy implications:

- **Data Summary:** An overview of the OWID dataset, its structural characteristics, and the foundational data used across the project.
- **A. Fundamentals:** Establishing the relationship between population, GDP, and emissions.
- **B. Trade & Intensity:** Examining whether emissions are truly falling in developed nations, or merely being exported via global supply chains.
- **C. Fuel & Energy:** Breaking down emissions by source (coal, oil, gas, land-use change) to understand structural dependencies.
- **D. Climate Justice:** A deep look into the disparity between historical cumulative responsibility and modern per-capita emissions.
- **E. Decoupling & GHG:** Investigating modern decoupling of GDP from emissions and broadening the scope to non-CO₂ greenhouse gases.
- **F. Hypothesis Testing:** Formal statistical validation of the visual arguments presented in the chapters.
- **Conclusion:** The final synthesis of all insights, summarizing the complete narrative.
